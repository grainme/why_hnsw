A fork is when one piece attacks two or more enemy pieces simultaneously. Knights are the most common forking piece. A pin occurs when an attacked piece cannot move because it would expose a more valuable piece behind it. A skewer is the reverse of a pin — the more valuable piece is in front and must move, exposing the piece behind. Discovered attacks happen when moving one piece reveals an attack from another piece behind it. Double check forces the king to move since two pieces give check simultaneously.
