The King's Indian Defense starts with 1.d4 Nf6 2.c4 g6. Black allows White to build a large pawn center, then counterattacks it. Black fianchettoes the dark-squared bishop to g7, aiming at the center and queenside. The Classical variation features a pawn storm on the kingside. The Samisch variation with f3 aims to build a massive center. The King's Indian leads to sharp, double-edged positions where both sides attack on opposite flanks.
