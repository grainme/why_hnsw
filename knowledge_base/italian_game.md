The Italian Game starts with 1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bc4. White develops the bishop to an active square targeting f7, the weakest point in Black's position. The Giuoco Piano (quiet game) continues with 3...Bc5 leading to slow positional play. The Evans Gambit sacrifices a pawn with 4.b4 for rapid development and attacking chances. The Italian Game is one of the oldest recorded openings in chess history.
