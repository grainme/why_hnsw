The Sicilian Defense begins with 1.e4 c5. It is the most popular response to White's 1.e4. Black immediately fights for the center with a flank pawn. The Sicilian leads to asymmetrical positions where both sides have chances to win. Major variations include the Najdorf, Dragon, and Scheveningen. The Najdorf variation with 5...a6 is considered the sharpest and most theoretically complex line in all of chess.
